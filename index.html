Car Rental

If you're flying in we recommend you rent a car, or team up with other guests to share a rental. It's not advised to try to use public transportation or ride-sharing for the whole weekend.

There are no train or bus lines that run to Stinson, and it can be difficult to match with an Uber/Lyft driver as it's removed from other towns by 30 minutes of (beautiful) winding roads.

Driving Expectations

Stinson Beach is about 1 hour from downtown San Francisco, and 30 min from neighboring towns like Mill Valley (without traffic). By distance it's not very far from the major destinations of the Bay Area, but because of the long stretch of two-lane winding roads it can take some time to make it all the way to Stinson. If you're not staying in Stinson for the weekend (no worries!) make sure you've double-checked the drive time to Willow

The roads get windy as you approach Stinson Beach, as a caution for all those who suffer from motion sickness (Dramamine may be a good idea). That said, the view on the drive is worth it!

Parking

There is limited parking on the Willow Camp Grounds which we will be reserving for guests staying with us on-site and for the wedding vendors. There is a very large parking lot in between the beach and Willow Camp which is about a 5 minute walk away. We will not have a shuttle service but if you need assistance or have to park far away, we will have a dedicated person to come pick you up and bring you to the venue. We highly recommend carpooling if you can. While you might be able to get an Uber/Lyft to Stinson Beach, getting one to pick you up there is very difficult.

accommodation

Stinson Beach is a very small town with limited places to stay. As with a lot of places around the Bay Area, things tend to book up fast.

Some of our close family members and friends will be staying at Willow Camp in either the Lodge or the Carriage House. Across the road from Willow Camp is the beautiful Sandpiper Lodging Hotel. We are currently inquiring about room availability, but feel free to go ahead and reserve a room here if this is where you want to stay.

Redwood Haus is a small B&B and is also *very* close to Willow Camp. This place is more rustic, very eclectic, but a bit cheaper than the Sandpiper. We would only recommend this to younger people who want to be close and donâ€™t mind a quirky experience.

You can also check out accommodations in Mill Valley (about a 30 minute drive to Stinson Beach) such as the Holiday Inn Express, Acqua Hotel, or Mill Valley Inn.

There are a lot of fun AirBnB's around Stinson Beach, Muir Beach, and Mill Valley.

You can also choose to stay in San Francisco which is about 45-60 minutes away. If you would like recommendations on what areas to stay in SF, feel free to reach out to us!
